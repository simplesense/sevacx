Mar 6 · Thursday · Local + Beauty Day (2nd visit)
🏮 Gwangjang Market → DDP → Myeongdong
ALL Morning — Gwangjang Market (10 min walk from hotel): One of Seoul's oldest and most authentic markets. Not touristy — real locals shop here. Great for browsing and breakfast.
SATYACARMEN Gwangjang food: Bindaetteok (mung bean pancake — eat one, skip the second), mayak gimbap (tiny rice rolls, low calorie), yukhoe (beef tartare, high protein). Ideal small-portion market format for both dietary regiments.
NATALIA Mid-morning — DDP (Dongdaemun Design Plaza): Right next to the hotel. Rotating design & art exhibitions, futuristic building that AI can't replicate IRL. Check what's showing — often has pop culture & gaming exhibitions.
ALL Also near hotel: Cheonggyecheon Stream — elevated urban stream walkway, good for a post-lunch walk. Peaceful contrast to the market energy.
CARMEN Afternoon — Myeongdong: Olive Young (flagship), Innisfree, Etude House, Laneige, COSRX, Sulwhasoo all within 2 blocks. 30–50% cheaper than US. Go on a weekday afternoon to avoid weekend crowds.
NATALIA Myeongdong underground shopping mall + street stalls — K-pop albums, character goods, anime accessories.
SATYA Dinner Myeongdong: Galbi or samgyeopsal — 2–3 pieces grilled meat with banchan. Skip or halve the rice. Works well for both Satya (GLP-1) and Carmen (dietary regiment).

🏯 Beijing Recommendations Mar 8–11 · 3 nights · Grand Hyatt Beijing · tag: BEIJING-REC

Mar 8 · Saturday · Arrival Afternoon
✈ KE855 lands ~12:30 PM → Hotel → Wangfujing Evening
ALL Airport → Hotel: Take Beijing Capital Airport Express (Line ABC) from T2/T3 to Dongzhimen (~16 min, ¥25/person), then 15-min taxi to Grand Hyatt Beijing. Total ~50 min, ~¥100–120 for the taxi leg. Or take a Didi (Uber equivalent) straight from arrivals: ~¥120–150, 45–60 min depending on traffic.
ALL Grand Hyatt Beijing is on East Chang'an Avenue in the Wangfujing district — one of the best locations in the city. Wangfujing Pedestrian Street is a literal 5-min walk from the lobby.
ALL Evening — Wangfujing Snack Street (Donghuamen Night Market, 5 min walk): Beijing's famous street food strip. Lamb chuanr (skewers) everywhere — this is the protein move. Also: stinky tofu, scallion pancakes, candied fruit (tanghulu). Lively, safe, walkable.
SATYACARMEN Lamb chuanr = ideal street food for both dietary regiments. 4–6 skewers = solid protein, naturally portioned. Cumin-spiced, high satiety. Get these first before browsing other stalls. Skip: fried dough, sweet red bean buns, full noodle bowls.
NATALIA Tanghulu (candied hawthorn on a stick) — Instagram must. Also watch for character merch stalls scattered along Wangfujing pedestrian street.
BEIJING-REC
Mar 9 · Sunday · Great Wall + Panjiayuan + Michelin Dinner
🧱 Mutianyu Great Wall → Panjiayuan Flea Market → DaDong Peking Duck
ALL Great Wall — Mutianyu Section (recommended over Badaling): 70 km from city, ~1.5 hrs by private car. Less crowded, more scenic, cable car up + toboggan ride down. Book a private car via hotel concierge or Klook (~¥500–700 for the car, split 3 ways = ¥170/person). Leave hotel by 7:30 AM. 2–3 hrs on the wall is plenty. Back in city by 1:30–2 PM. mutianyugreatwall.com · Cable car ¥140 round trip · Toboggan ¥100.
NATALIA Toboggan ride down the Wall — non-negotiable. Metal luge track down the mountain, you control the speed. One of the most fun things you can do in China. She will love this.
NATALIA Afternoon — Panjiayuan Antique & Flea Market (Sunday = full market): One of Asia's largest flea markets — vintage Mao posters, jade, calligraphy, quirky toys, retro tech, anime-adjacent merch, cheap prints. Budget ¥200–500 for a great haul. Prices start high; negotiate to 30–40% of asking. 30 min from the Wall on the drive back. Open 4:30 AM–6 PM on weekends. Sunday only at full capacity — perfect timing.
ALL Dinner — DaDong Roast Duck (Michelin-recognised, consistently ranked among China's best restaurants): Signature lean-cut Peking duck — lower fat than traditional, stunning presentation. Book in advance via their app or hotel concierge. Expect ¥300–450/person. The Tuanjiehu branch or Nanxincang branch are both excellent. dadongchina.com
SATYACARMEN DaDong dietary strategy: Order the duck (2–3 slices per person is satisfying), eat with minimal pancake, use plum sauce sparingly. The crispy skin is the star — small amounts very satiating. Also order: cucumber salad, tofu dish, skip the sesame buns. Lean Peking duck works well for both dietary regiments.
BEIJING-REC
Mar 10 · Monday · Hutong + 798 Art District + Ghost Street
🏮 Nanluoguxiang Hutong → 798 Art District → Guijie (Ghost Street) Dinner
ALL Morning — Nanluoguxiang Hutong: Beijing's most charming historic alleyway — 800 metres of indie boutiques, local snacks, courtyard cafés, and craft shops. Not a tourist trap: locals actually live and work in the surrounding hutong network. Great for a relaxed morning browse. 20 min taxi from Grand Hyatt.
NATALIA Hutong indie shops sell handmade pins, quirky stationery, vintage anime-style prints, and local illustration art. Budget ¥100–200 for a fun haul. Also: Guozijian Street nearby has antique book stalls with retro Japanese manga imports.
CARMEN Hutong beauty boutiques carry local Chinese skincare brands (Florasis, Proya, CHANDO) at a fraction of US prices. Worth a browse alongside Nanluoguxiang's indie lifestyle stores.
ALL Afternoon — 798 Art District: Beijing's former military factory turned contemporary art hub. 50+ galleries, large-scale sculpture installations, street art, design shops. The merch in the design stores is genuinely unique — illustration prints, art books, designer toys. Good 2–3 hrs. 30 min taxi from Hutong. Free entry to most galleries.
NATALIA 798 has multiple stores selling limited-edition art toys (Pop Mart style but independent), anime-adjacent illustration prints from Chinese artists, and designer stationery. This is the hidden gem for her — quality beats quantity here.
ALL Optional quick detour — Temple of Heaven: If time allows between Hutong and 798 (it's roughly on the route). Stunning circular altar, takes only 45 min. Worth it for the visual impact. ¥30 entry.
ALL Dinner — Guijie (Ghost Street): Beijing's famous restaurant street — lit up with red lanterns at night. Mala hot pot or chuanr skewer restaurants line the full kilometre. Loud, communal, very Beijing. Best for a final big group dinner before train day.
SATYACARMEN Ghost Street dietary strategy: Order a chuanr platter (lamb, beef, mushroom, tofu skewers — all protein/veg forward). At hot pot: go with broth-cooked items (thinly sliced lamb, beef, tofu, leafy greens), avoid: fried items, starchy add-ons, sesame paste in large quantities. Mala broth stimulates appetite — both eat slowly and stop early.
