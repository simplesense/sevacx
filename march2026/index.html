🗺️ Seoul Recommendations Mar 5–8 · tag: SEOUL-REC

Mar 5 · Wednesday · Arrival Night
🛬 Arrive ~7 PM — Airport → Hotel → Rest
ALL Airport Limousine Bus — Route 6001 · ICN Terminal 2, B1 Floor, Stop 29 · Drops at Dongdaemun/Myeongdong area · ₩17,000 adults / ₩12,000 children (6–12) · Runs every 10–15 min, last bus 10:49 PM · airportlimousine.co.kr
ALL After check-in, short walk to Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP) — Zaha Hadid's landmark is lit up at night, 5 min from hotel. Zero effort, impressive intro. Good for stretch after long flight.
SATYACARMEN Dinner: Sundubu jjigae or doenjang jjigae near the hotel. Small protein-forward portions — ideal first night after long-haul (GLP-1 + Carmen's dietary regiment). Avoid heavy or fried food.
ALL Early bed. Seoul is +17hrs from PHX — jet lag hits hardest on day 1.
SEOUL-REC
Mar 6 · Thursday · Markets + Design + Beauty Day
🏮 Gwangjang Market → 🏛 DDP → Myeongdong
ALL Morning — Gwangjang Market (10 min walk from hotel): One of Seoul's oldest and most authentic markets. Not touristy — real locals shop here. Great for browsing and breakfast.
SATYACARMEN Gwangjang food: Bindaetteok (mung bean pancake — eat one, skip the second), mayak gimbap (tiny rice rolls, low calorie), yukhoe (beef tartare, high protein). Ideal small-portion market format for both dietary regiments.
ALL Afternoon — DDP (Dongdaemun Design Plaza): 5 min walk from hotel. Zaha Hadid's landmark building — futuristic architecture, free outdoor plazas, rotating exhibitions, design shops. Cheonggyecheon Stream walkway connects Gwangjang to DDP for a scenic stroll in between.
NATALIA DDP has K-culture pop-up shops and design galleries she'll enjoy — also a good photo spot. From DDP it's a short walk or subway to Myeongdong for the evening.
CARMEN Evening — Myeongdong: Olive Young (flagship), Innisfree, Etude House, Laneige, COSRX, Sulwhasoo all within 2 blocks. 30–50% cheaper than US. Weekday evening = less crowded.
NATALIA Myeongdong underground mall + street stalls — K-pop albums, character goods, anime accessories. Great evening wind-down after a full market + design day.
SATYA Dinner Myeongdong: Galbi or samgyeopsal — 2–3 pieces grilled meat with banchan. Skip or halve the rice. Works well for both dietary regiments.
SEOUL-REC
Mar 7 · Friday · Anime + Gangnam Day
🎌 Hongdae → COEX Mall → Gangnam Dinner
NATALIA Hongdae (all morning): Seoul's anime/manga hub — Animeta store, multiple doujinshi shops, indie K-pop merch, manhwa bang (manga cafes). She could spend the full day here.
CARMEN Hongdae indie beauty boutiques — Korean brands not found in Myeongdong (Tamburins, Aestura, local indie labels).
ALL Afternoon — COEX Mall, Gangnam: Starfield Library (landmark atrium bookstore). Bandi & Luni's has dedicated manga/manhwa floors for Natalia.
SATYA Dinner Gangnam: Hanjeongsik (Korean multi-course set) — many small dishes, eat what you want, leave the rest. Best format for both dietary regiments in Korean cuisine. Or upscale galbi restaurants here.
SEOUL-REC

🏯 Beijing Recommendations Mar 8–11 · 3 nights · Grand Hyatt Beijing · tag: BEIJING-REC

Mar 8 · Saturday · Arrival Afternoon
✈ KE855 lands ~12:30 PM → Hotel → Wangfujing Evening
ALL Airport → Hotel: Take Beijing Capital Airport Express (Line ABC) from T2/T3 to Dongzhimen (~16 min, ¥25/person), then 15-min taxi to Grand Hyatt Beijing. Total ~50 min, ~¥100–120 for the taxi leg. Or take a Didi (Uber equivalent) straight from arrivals: ~¥120–150, 45–60 min depending on traffic.
ALL Grand Hyatt Beijing is on East Chang'an Avenue in the Wangfujing district — one of the best locations in the city. Wangfujing Pedestrian Street is a literal 5-min walk from the lobby.
ALL Evening — Wangfujing Snack Street (Donghuamen Night Market, 5 min walk): Beijing's famous street food strip. Lamb chuanr (skewers) everywhere — this is the protein move. Also: stinky tofu, scallion pancakes, candied fruit (tanghulu). Lively, safe, walkable.
SATYACARMEN Lamb chuanr = ideal street food for both dietary regiments. 4–6 skewers = solid protein, naturally portioned. Cumin-spiced, high satiety. Get these first before browsing other stalls. Skip: fried dough, sweet red bean buns, full noodle bowls.
NATALIA Tanghulu (candied hawthorn on a stick) — Instagram must. Also watch for character merch stalls scattered along Wangfujing pedestrian street.
BEIJING-REC
Mar 9 · Sunday · Great Wall + Panjiayuan + Michelin Dinner
🧱 Mutianyu Great Wall → Panjiayuan Flea Market → DaDong Peking Duck
ALL Great Wall — Mutianyu Section (recommended over Badaling): 70 km from city, ~1.5 hrs by private car. Less crowded, more scenic, cable car up + toboggan ride down. Book a private car via hotel concierge or Klook (~¥500–700 for the car, split 3 ways = ¥170/person). Leave hotel by 7:30 AM. 2–3 hrs on the wall is plenty. Back in city by 1:30–2 PM. mutianyugreatwall.com · Cable car ¥140 round trip · Toboggan ¥100.
NATALIA Toboggan ride down the Wall — non-negotiable. Metal luge track down the mountain, you control the speed. One of the most fun things you can do in China. She will love this.
NATALIA Afternoon — Panjiayuan Antique & Flea Market (Sunday = full market): One of Asia's largest flea markets — vintage Mao posters, jade, calligraphy, quirky toys, retro tech, anime-adjacent merch, cheap prints. Budget ¥200–500 for a great haul. Prices start high; negotiate to 30–40% of asking. 30 min from the Wall on the drive back. Open 4:30 AM–6 PM on weekends. Sunday only at full capacity — perfect timing.
ALL Dinner — DaDong Roast Duck (Michelin-recognised, consistently ranked among China's best restaurants): Signature lean-cut Peking duck — lower fat than traditional, stunning presentation. Book in advance via their app or hotel concierge. Expect ¥300–450/person. The Tuanjiehu branch or Nanxincang branch are both excellent. dadongchina.com
SATYACARMEN DaDong dietary strategy: Order the duck (2–3 slices per person is satisfying), eat with minimal pancake, use plum sauce sparingly. The crispy skin is the star — small amounts very satiating. Also order: cucumber salad, tofu dish, skip the sesame buns. Lean Peking duck works well for both dietary regiments.
BEIJING-REC
Mar 10 · Monday · Hutong + 798 Art District + Ghost Street
🏮 Nanluoguxiang Hutong → 798 Art District → Guijie (Ghost Street) Dinner
ALL Morning — Nanluoguxiang Hutong: Beijing's most charming historic alleyway — 800 metres of indie boutiques, local snacks, courtyard cafés, and craft shops. Not a tourist trap: locals actually live and work in the surrounding hutong network. Great for a relaxed morning browse. 20 min taxi from Grand Hyatt.
NATALIA Hutong indie shops sell handmade pins, quirky stationery, vintage anime-style prints, and local illustration art. Budget ¥100–200 for a fun haul. Also: Guozijian Street nearby has antique book stalls with retro Japanese manga imports.
CARMEN Hutong beauty boutiques carry local Chinese skincare brands (Florasis, Proya, CHANDO) at a fraction of US prices. Worth a browse alongside Nanluoguxiang's indie lifestyle stores.
ALL Afternoon — 798 Art District: Beijing's former military factory turned contemporary art hub. 50+ galleries, large-scale sculpture installations, street art, design shops. The merch in the design stores is genuinely unique — illustration prints, art books, designer toys. Good 2–3 hrs. 30 min taxi from Hutong. Free entry to most galleries.
NATALIA 798 has multiple stores selling limited-edition art toys (Pop Mart style but independent), anime-adjacent illustration prints from Chinese artists, and designer stationery. This is the hidden gem for her — quality beats quantity here.
ALL Optional quick detour — Temple of Heaven: If time allows between Hutong and 798 (it's roughly on the route). Stunning circular altar, takes only 45 min. Worth it for the visual impact. ¥30 entry.
ALL Dinner — Guijie (Ghost Street): Beijing's famous restaurant street — lit up with red lanterns at night. Mala hot pot or chuanr skewer restaurants line the full kilometre. Loud, communal, very Beijing. Best for a final big group dinner before train day.
SATYACARMEN Ghost Street dietary strategy: Order a chuanr platter (lamb, beef, mushroom, tofu skewers — all protein/veg forward). At hot pot: go with broth-cooked items (thinly sliced lamb, beef, tofu, leafy greens), avoid: fried items, starchy add-ons, sesame paste in large quantities. Mala broth stimulates appetite — both eat slowly and stop early.
BEIJING-REC
Mar 11 · Tuesday · Checkout Day → Train to Shanghai
🚄 Checkout Noon → Beijing South Station → Pudong Shangri-La
ALL Settle hotel bill night before (Mar 10 evening). Store bags at Grand Hyatt bell desk on Mar 11 morning.
ALL Morning (9–11 AM): Final Wangfujing walk — pick up any last items from the pedestrian street or Beijing Apm mall (attached to Grand Hyatt). Last chance for street snacks.
ALL Train to Shanghai: High-speed G-train from Beijing South Station (南京南, Nanjing South is different — use 北京南, Beijing Nan). Fastest trains (G1/G3) take 4 hrs 30 min. Didi/taxi to Beijing South Station: ~45 min, ¥80–100. Book via 12306.cn or hotel concierge. Business class recommended for the 3 of you (~¥900–1100/person vs ~¥550 second class). Arrives Shanghai Hongqiao — taxi to Pudong Shangri-La ~60 min.
SATYACARMEN Train food options are poor. Pack a light hotel breakfast box (ask concierge night before) or eat at the station before boarding. Both dietary regiments do fine on trains — low appetite, just water and a small snack.

🌆 Shanghai Recommendations Mar 11–16 · 5 nights · Pudong Shangri-La · tag: SHANGHAI-REC

Mar 11 · Tuesday · Arrival from Beijing
🚄 Train Arrives → Check-in → The Bund at Night
ALL Train → hotel: Beijing Nan → Shanghai Hongqiao (~4.5 hrs). From Hongqiao station take Metro Line 2 east direct to Lujiazui station (~35 min). Pudong Shangri-La is a 3-min walk from the exit — easy with luggage.
ALL Evening — The Bund: 15-min walk from hotel or take the Bund Sightseeing Tunnel (¥55 round trip, novelty). Best after dark — the colonial buildings on the west bank are fully lit, and the Pudong towers are reflected in the river. The view from The Bund of Pudong (where you're staying) is the signature Shanghai shot.
SATYACARMEN Arrival dinner near hotel: Nadaman (in-hotel, Japanese) for easy high-protein meal, or Hai Di Lao hot pot (5-min walk, Lujiazui IFC branch) — thinly sliced lamb/beef in clear broth, load on tofu and greens, skip the noodle add-on.
SHANGHAI-REC
Mar 12 · Wednesday · Pudong Skyline Day
🗼 Shanghai Tower (118F) → Lujiazui Walk → Huangpu Riverside
ALL Shanghai Tower observation deck: 632m, 2nd tallest building in the world. Floors 118–119. Book online in advance: ¥180/person. 10-min walk from hotel — you can see it from your room. ~90 min up top including the world's fastest elevator (20 seconds, 580m/min). Morning = clearest views.
NATALIA K11 MUSEA / IFC Mall (Lujiazui): 5-min walk from the towers. K11 MUSEA has rotating interactive art installations on multiple floors plus K-pop pop-up events. IFC bookstore floor carries manga and art books. Good 1-hour browse.
ALL Afternoon — Lujiazui free walk: The three towers (Shanghai Tower, SWFC "bottle opener," Jin Mao) form a triangle — walk the plazas between them, all free. Huangpu riverfront path runs from Lujiazui all the way back to the hotel.
SATYACARMEN Evening dinner: Lost Heaven (云南风味) on Yan'an East Road near The Bund — Yunnan cuisine: wild mushroom hot pot, baked cured Yunnan meats, steamed fish with herbal broth. Naturally high-protein, low-carb leaning. Reserve ahead.
SHANGHAI-REC
Mar 13 · Thursday · Old Town + Nanjing Road
🏯 Yu Garden (early) → Yuyuan Bazaar → Nanjing Road
ALL Yu Garden — arrive 8:30am sharp: Classic Ming-dynasty Suzhou-style garden, 16th century. Ticket ¥40/person. Gets extremely packed after 10am — arriving at open is the only way to see it without crowds. ~1.5 hrs inside. Metro Line 10 to Yuyuan Garden station.
SATYACARMEN Yuyuan Bazaar food: Nanxiang Mantou Dian (south gate, established 1900) — original Shanghai xiaolongbao. 4 pieces max for Satya, assess for Carmen. Avoid the fried sesame balls and candied hawthorn stalls. Shengjianbao (pan-fried bun) 2 pieces is fine.
NATALIA Yuyuan Bazaar stalls: Chinese novelty goods, tea sets, jade trinkets, character snacks, hair accessories. Budget ¥200–300. The alleys behind the main tourist strip (Fuyou Road area) have cheaper finds and fewer crowds.
ALL Afternoon — Nanjing Road pedestrian street: 1.2km, very touristy but a box to tick. Department stores (Shimao, Raffles City). A tram runs the full length if feet are tired (~¥2). It dead-ends at The Bund — good spot for sunset before dinner.
CARMEN Sephora + Watsons flagship on Nanjing Road. Wuyou Road (one block off Nanjing) has Chinese domestic beauty brands not available in the US — worth a detour.
SHANGHAI-REC
Mar 14 · Friday · French Concession + Tianzifang
🌿 Wukang Road → Tianzifang → Xintiandi Dinner
ALL Morning — Wukang Road (武康路): Shanghai's most photographed street. Tree-lined Art Deco boulevard from the French Concession era. Wukang Mansion (fan-shaped 1924 building at the main junction) is the hero shot. Walk south → Fuxing Road → Fuxing Park (free, locals doing tai chi, very peaceful). Metro Line 10 to Jiaotong University station.
NATALIA Tianzifang (田子坊) — 2–3 hours: Indie boutiques in a maze of brick alleyways near Taikang Lu. Handmade jewelry, anime-adjacent art prints, quirky stationery, character enamel pins, alternative/streetwear fashion, independent illustrators. More authentic and cheaper than tourist shops elsewhere. Budget ¥300–500. Metro Line 9 Dapuqiao station.
CARMEN Anfu Road and Changshu Road (within the French Concession) have small Chinese fashion concept stores worth browsing for design inspiration. Xintiandi Style mall nearby has contemporary Chinese fashion brands.
ALL Evening — Xintiandi: Renovated shikumen (stone-gate) block turned upscale dining district. Jesse Restaurant (本家上海菜) for elevated Shanghainese: smoked fish cold platter, 2–3 pieces braised pork belly (hong shao rou), stir-fried bamboo shoots. Reserve ahead. 15-min walk from Tianzifang.
SHANGHAI-REC
Mar 15 · Saturday · Natalia Day — Shanghai Disneyland
🎡 Shanghai Disneyland — Book Online in Advance
ALL Shanghai Disneyland: Metro Line 11 from Lujiazui to Disneyland station, ~30 min. Tickets ¥499–749/person (weekend = higher tier pricing). Book online 1–2 weeks ahead — weekends sell out. The castle (Enchanted Storybook) is the largest in any Disney park globally. shanghaidisneyresort.com
NATALIA Must-do: TRON Lightcycle Power Run (headline coaster, unique to this park — use Lightning Lane, lines 90+ min otherwise). Pirates of the Caribbean: Battle for the Sunken Treasure (best version of this ride in the world — massive show scenes). Zootopia world (2023 expansion, very well done). Toy Story Hotel Land for character meets.
SATYACARMEN Park food: Smaller portions than US parks — naturally friendly to both dietary regiments. Grilled chicken skewers in Tomorrowland, dim sum at Wandering Moon Teahouse, barbecue at Adventureland. Skip the bubble waffle cones and Minnie/Mickey buns (pure sugar + dough).
ALL Alternate if Disneyland not preferred: Qibao Water Town (七宝古镇) — ancient water town 30 min by metro, free to walk, authentic old Shanghai canal atmosphere, good street food, far less touristy. Or use Mar 15 for a spa day at Shangri-La + last Shanghai shopping.
SHANGHAI-REC
Mar 16 · Sunday · Checkout → Train to Guangzhou
🚄 Shanghai Hongqiao → Guangzhou South (~5.5 hrs)
ALL Checkout logistics: Settle bill night before (Mar 15 evening). Store bags at Shangri-La bell desk morning. Metro Line 2 west from Lujiazui to Hongqiao Railway Station (~35 min) — allow 30 min inside the station (it's enormous). Book bags/oversized storage at station if needed.
ALL Train timing: G-train (高铁) Shanghai Hongqiao → Guangzhou South. Recommended: G79 (departs 10:58am, arrives Guangzhou South ~4:30pm) or G97 (departs 9:00am, arrives ~2:30pm). Business class ¥1,100–1,300/person — lie-flat seats, power outlets. Book via Trip.com or 12306.cn.

🏙 Guangzhou Recommendations Mar 16–20 · 4 nights · Carmen's prototype work base · tag: GUANGZHOU-REC

Mar 16 · Sunday · Arrival from Shanghai
🚄 Arrive Guangzhou South → Check-in → Rest Night
ALL From Guangzhou South station: Metro Line 2 north into the city (15–25 min to Tianhe/central area). Guangzhou South is in Panyu — not central, so build in 30–40 min transit. Suggest hotel in Tianhe District (central, Metro Line 3 connects to everything) or near Haizhu (fabric market area for Carmen).
ALL Hotel options (see Open Items — unbooked): Guangzhou Marriott Hotel Tianhe (central, ~¥950/night) · Four Seasons Guangzhou (Zhujiang New Town, upscale) · Shangri-La Guangzhou (riverside, near Shamian Island, ~¥1,100/night) · Ask Grand Hyatt Beijing concierge for World of Hyatt cross-chain rates before departure.
ALL Arrival dinner: Guangzhou is the birthplace of Cantonese cuisine — any neighborhood congee house is excellent. Cantonese wonton noodle soup (eat wontons, leave half the noodles) = ideal light post-travel meal.
GUANGZHOU-REC
Mar 17 · Monday · Dim Sum + Shamian Island + Chen Clan
🥟 Guangzhou Restaurant → Shamian Island → Chen Clan Academy
CARMEN Carmen's work base — Guangzhou International Textile City (中大纺织城): China's largest fabric wholesale market in Haizhu District, near Zhongshan University South Gate. 200+ buildings across multiple blocks. Metro Line 8 to Wanshengwei station. Most vendors open 8am–5pm weekdays. Bring samples, specs, and photos.
ALL Breakfast: Guangzhou Restaurant (广州酒家): Wenchang Road, Liwan District. Opens 7am. The most famous dim sum institution in the city — real locals, not tourists. Order: har gow (shrimp), siu mai (pork/shrimp), cheung fun (rice noodle rolls), century egg & pork congee. ~¥80/person for a full spread.
SATYA Shamian Island (沙面岛): Former French and British colonial concession — 2km² island of European-style villas, banyan trees, and quiet cobblestone streets. Free to walk, no ticket. Perfect 1.5-hour stroll. Benches everywhere — bring a book. Near Liwan District.
SATYANATALIA Chen Clan Academy (陈家祠): Qing Dynasty (1894) ancestral hall — the finest example of Cantonese folk architecture anywhere. Intricate brick relief carvings, ceramic roof sculptures, stone and woodwork covering every surface. Ticket ¥10. Metro Line 8 Chenjiaci station. ~2 hours. Not to miss.
ALL Evening: Canton Tower (广州塔): 600m. LED light show runs daily at 8pm — free to watch from the riverside Flower City Square below. Observation deck (¥200+) optional. Pearl River riverside walk is free and beautiful at night.
GUANGZHOU-REC
Mar 18 · Tuesday · Natalia Day — Chimelong Paradise
🎢 Chimelong Paradise — Satya + Natalia Full Day
CARMEN Carmen: Full day at fabric market / factory visits in Panyu or Foshan. Best day for substantive prototype work — Satya and Natalia will be occupied all day.
SATYANATALIA Chimelong Paradise (长隆欢乐世界): One of Asia's top-ranked theme parks in Panyu (same area as Guangzhou South station). Metro Line 3 south to Hanxi Changlong station, then free shuttle bus (~5 min). Ticket ~¥320/person. Open 10am–8pm weekdays. chimelong.com
NATALIA Must-do rides: Jungle Trailblazer (10-inversion coaster, held a world record), Flying Tigers (inverted coaster), Speed (racing dual coaster), Terror Castle (dark haunted ride). Also: Chimelong International Circus evening show (~¥220 separate, 2-hour world-class acrobatics + animals) — book in advance if interested.
ALL Bonus — adjacent Chimelong Safari Park (长隆野生动物世界): Panda breeding center, white tigers, African savanna section, koalas. Opens 9am (before Paradise). Combo ticket available. Families with younger-age energy can do both in one day — Safari in the morning, Paradise after lunch.
GUANGZHOU-REC
Mar 19 · Wednesday · Last Day + Pearl River Cruise
🛍 Beijing Road + Guangdong Museum + Pearl River Night Cruise
ALL Morning: Beijing Road (北京路步行街): Guangzhou's main pedestrian shopping strip — less touristy than Nanjing Road in Shanghai, better prices. At the main intersection: excavated Song and Ming dynasty road layers visible through a glass floor panel in the pavement (free, fascinating). Good for last-day trinkets and gifts.
